FCFS Scheduling Algorithm

Definition : FCFS is a non-preemptive, simple and easy-to-implement CPU scheduling algorithm.It assigns the CPU tot the process that arrives first in the ready queue and executes it until its completion.


Key characteristics :

1.non-preemptive : Once a process is allocated to the CPU, it continues to execute until it completes or terminates.

2.FIFO(First-In-First-Out) queue : Processes are served in the order they arrive in the ready queue.

3.Simple : Easy to implement and understand.

4.No priority : All processes are treated equally, with no consideration for priority or urgency.

Example :

Suppose we have five processes(P1-P5) with arrival times and burst times as follows :

Process Arrival Time(AT) Burst Time(BT)
P1 0 3
P2 1 2
P3 2 6
P4 4 4
P5 5 2

Using FCFS, the scheduling order would be :

P1(arrives 1st, executes for 3 time units)

P2(arrives 2nd, executes for 2 time units)

P3(arrives 3rd, executes for 6 time units)

P4(arrives 4th, executes for 4 time units)

P5(arrives 5th, executes for 2 time units)


Advantages :
1.Simple to implement and understand
2.Easy to manage context switching.


Disadvantages :
1.Starvation : Low-priority processes may never get executed if high-priority processes keep arriving.
2.Convey Effect : A sequence of short-burst processes can lead to inefficient CPU utilization.
3.Not suitable for time-sharing systems,as it does not provide fair allocation of CPU time.
